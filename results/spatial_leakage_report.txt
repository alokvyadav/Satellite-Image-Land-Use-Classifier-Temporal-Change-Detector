=== Spatial Leakage Experiment ===

Random Split Validation Accuracy: 0.9731 (F1: 0.9719)
Spatial Block Split Validation Accuracy: 0.9530 (F1: 0.9343)
Spatial Leakage Accuracy Gap: 2.01 percentage points

Explanation:
In satellite imagery, neighboring pixels or tiles are highly spatially autocorrelated.
When performing a random split, tiles from the same geographic coordinates or neighborhoods
are placed into both the train and validation sets. The model essentially memorizes the
surroundings of these tiles rather than learning generalized features. Thus, the random split
accuracy is artificially inflated. The spatial block split, which partitions the dataset
geographically (e.g. by longitudes), is a more rigorous and realistic measure of how the model
generalizes to unseen geographical regions.
